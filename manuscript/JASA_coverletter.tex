Dear Editors: \newline


I would like to submit our manuscript
``Deep Ranking with Heterogeneous Effects"
by my coauthors  Yuanhang Luo, Shuxing Fang, Ruijian Han, and myself,
to the \textit{Journal of the American Statistical Association} for possible publication.\\

This submission presents new developments in statistical ranking models by integrating dynamic covariate information and leveraging neural network architectures to approximate contextual effects. It addresses a key limitation of classical latent-score ranking models, which fail to distinguish objects’ intrinsic scores from contextual effects that are typically nonlinear and can dominate observed outcomes.

Specifically, we introduce a semiparametric ranking framework in which the log-score of each object is modeled as the sum of a utility parameter and a nonparametric covariate effect. Within this framework, we establish model identifiability under mild regularity and connectivity conditions. For estimation, we approximate the covariate effect using a neural network and estimate the parameters via maximum likelihood. Under random design assumptions, we show that the resulting estimator exists with high probability and derive non-asymptotic error bounds that achieve minimax optimality for both the parametric and nonparametric components. We also validate our results on synthetic data and apply the proposed model to a recent ATP tennis dataset, where it reveals interesting dynamic patterns among several current top players. \\
