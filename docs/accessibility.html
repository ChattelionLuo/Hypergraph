Accessibility

Accessibility statement.

Project Hypergraph aims to make research and probability demonstrations readable, navigable, and usable across devices.

Our approach

We use semantic HTML, responsive layouts, descriptive link text, scalable typography, and keyboard-accessible navigation patterns. We will continue improving contrast, labels, image alternatives, and mathematical content presentation as the site evolves.

Mathematical and visual material

Some research pages include formulas, figures, and probability visualizations. Where practical, we pair these materials with explanatory text so the core meaning remains available even when a visual asset is incomplete or difficult to access.

Responsible content note

Probability demonstrations on this website are educational and research-oriented. They are not betting strategies, gambling advice, wagering recommendations, or gambling services.

Feedback

If you encounter an accessibility barrier, contact contact@projecthypergraph.com and describe the page, device, and issue.
